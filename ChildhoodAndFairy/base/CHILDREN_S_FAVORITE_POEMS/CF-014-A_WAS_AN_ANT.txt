CHILDREN'S FAVORITE POEMS
A WAS AN ANT

1. A was an ant Who seldom stood still, And who made a nice house In the side of a hill.

2. a Nice little ant!

3. B was a book With a binding of blue, And pictures and stories For me and for you.

4. b Nice little book!

5. C was a cat Who ran after a rat; But his courage did fail When she seized on his tail.

6. c Crafty old cat!

7. D was a duck With spots on his back, Who lived in the water, And always said "Quack!" d Dear little duck!

8. E was an elephant, Stately and wise: He had tusks and a trunk, And two queer little eyes, e Oh, what funny small eyes!

9. F was a fish Who was caught in a net; But he got out again, And is quite alive yet.

10. f Lively young fish!

11. G was a goat Who was spotted with brown: When he did not lie still He walked up and down.

12. g Good little goat!

13. H was a hat Which was all on one side; Its crown was too high, And its brim was too wide.

14. h Oh, what a hat!

15. I was some ice So white and so nice, But which nobody tasted; And so it was wasted.

16. i All that good ice!

17. J was a jackdaw Who hopped up and dowa In the principal street Of a neighboring town.

18. j All through the town!

19. K was a kite Which flew out of sight, Above houses so high, Quite into the sky.

20. k Fly away, kite!

21. L was a light Which burned all the night, And lighted the gloom Of a very dark room.

22. l Useful nice light!

23. M was a mill Which stood on a hill, And turned round and round With a loud hummy sound.

24. m Useful old mill!

25. N was a net Which was thrown in the sea To catch fish for dinner For you and for me.

26. n Nice little net!

27. O was an orange So yellow and round: When it fell off the tree, It fell down to the ground; o Down to the ground!

28. P was a pig, Who was not very big; But his tail was too curly, And that made him surly.

29. p Cross little pig!

30. Q was a quail With a very short tail; And he fed upon corn In the evening and morn.

31. q Quaint little quail!

32. R was a rabbit, Who had a bad habit Of eating the flowers In gardens and bowers.

33. r Naughty fat rabbit!

34. S was the sugar-tongs, Nippity-nee, To take up the sugar To put in our tea.

35. s Nippity-nee!

36. T was a tortoise, All yellow and black: He walked slowly away, And he never came back.

37. t Torty never came back!

38. U was an urn All polished and bright, And full of hot water At noon and at night.

39. u Useful old urn!

40. V was a villa Which stood on a hill, By the side of a river, And close to a mill.

41. v Nice little villa!

42. W was a whale With a very long tail, Whose movements were frantic Across the Atlantic.

43. w Monstrous old whale!

44. X was King Xerxes, Who, more than all Turks is, Renowned for his fashion Of fury and passion.

45. x Angry old Xerxes!

46. Y was a yew, Which flourished and grew By a quiet abode Near the side of a road.

47. y Dark little yew!

48. Z was some zinc, So shiny and bright, Which caused you to wink In the sun's merry light.

49. z Beautiful zinc!

50. EDWARD LEAR
