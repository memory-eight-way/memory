OLD-FASHIONED POEMS
LIMERICKS

1. There was an Old Man in a tree, Who was horribly bored by a Bee; When they said, "Does it buzz?" he replied, "Yes, it does!

2. It's a regular brute of a Bee."

3. There was an Old Man on some rocks, Who shut his Wife up in a box: When she said, "Let me out," he exclaimed, "Without doubt You will pass all your life in that box."

4. There was an Old Man who said "How Shall I flee from this horrible Cow?

5. I will sit on this stile, and continue to smile, Which may soften the heart of that Cow."

6. There was an Old Man who said, "Hush!

7. I perceive a young bird in this bush!"

8. When they said, "Is it small?" he replied, "Not at all; It is four times as big as the bush!"

9. There was once an Old Man with a beard, Who said, "It is just as I feared!-- Two Owls and a Hen, Four Larks and a Wren Have all built their nests in my beard."

10. There was an old person of Ware Who rode on the back of a bear; When they said, "Does it trot?"

11. He said, "Certainly not, It's a Moppsikon Floppsikon bear."

12. There was a young lady in blue, Who said, "Is it you?

13. Is it you?"

14. When they said, "Yes, it is," she replied only, "Whizz!"

15. That ungracious young lady in blue.

16. EDWARD LEAR
