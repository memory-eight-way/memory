CHILDREN'S FAVORITE POEMS
THE THREE LITTLE KITTENS

1. (A CAT'S TALE, WITH ADDITIONS) Three little kittens lost their mittens; And they began to cry, O mother dear, We very much fear That we have lost our mittens.

2. Lost your mittens!

3. You naughty kittens!

4. Then you shall have no pie.

5. Mee-ow, mee-ow, mee-ow.

6. No, you shall have no pie.

7. Mee-ow, mee-ow, mee-ow.

8. The three little kittens found their mittens, And they began to cry, O mother dear, See here, see here; See, we have found our mittens.

9. Put on your mittens, You silly kittens, And you may have some pie.

10. Purr-r, purr-r, purr-r, O let us have the pie.

11. Purr-r, purr-r, purr-r.

12. The three little kittens put on their mittens, And soon ate up the pie; O mother dear, We greatly fear That we have soiled our mittens.

13. Soiled your mittens!

14. You naughty kittens!

15. Then they began to sigh, Mee-ow, mee-ow, mee-ow, Then they began to sigh.

16. Mee-ow, mee-ow, mee-ow.

17. The three little kittens washed their mittens, And hung them out to dry; O mother dear, Do not you hear, That we have washed our mittens?

18. Washed your mittens!

19. O, you're good kittens.

20. But I smell a rat close by; Hush!

21. hush!

22. mee-ow, mee-ow.

23. We smell a rat close by, Mee-ow, mee-ow, mee-ow.

24. ELIZA LEE FOLLEN
