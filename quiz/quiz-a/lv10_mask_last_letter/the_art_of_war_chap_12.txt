XII. THE ATTACK BY FIRE
XII. TH_ ATTAC_ B_ FIR_

1-1. Sun Tzu said: There are five ways of attacking with fire.
1-1. Su_ Tz_ said_ Ther_ ar_ fiv_ way_ o_ attackin_ wit_ fire_

1-2. The first is to burn soldiers in their camp; the second is to burn stores; the third is to burn baggage trains; the fourth is to burn arsenals and magazines; the fifth is to hurl dropping fire amongst the enemy.
1-2. Th_ firs_ i_ t_ bur_ soldier_ i_ thei_ camp_ th_ secon_ i_ t_ bur_ stores_ th_ thir_ i_ t_ bur_ baggag_ trains_ th_ fourt_ i_ t_ bur_ arsenal_ an_ magazines_ th_ fift_ i_ t_ hur_ droppin_ fir_ amongs_ th_ enemy_

2-1. In order to carry out an attack, we must have means available.
2-1. I_ orde_ t_ carr_ ou_ a_ attack_ w_ mus_ hav_ mean_ available_

2-2. The material for raising fire should always be kept in readiness.
2-2. Th_ materia_ fo_ raisin_ fir_ shoul_ alway_ b_ kep_ i_ readiness_

3. There is a proper season for making attacks with fire, and special days for starting a conflagration.
3. Ther_ i_ _ prope_ seaso_ fo_ makin_ attack_ wit_ fire_ an_ specia_ day_ fo_ startin_ _ conflagration_

4. The proper season is when the weather is very dry; the special days are those when the moon is in the constellations of the Sieve, the Wall, the Wing or the Cross-bar; for these four are all days of rising wind.
4. Th_ prope_ seaso_ i_ whe_ th_ weathe_ i_ ver_ dry_ th_ specia_ day_ ar_ thos_ whe_ th_ moo_ i_ i_ th_ constellation_ o_ th_ Sieve_ th_ Wall_ th_ Win_ o_ th_ Cross-bar_ fo_ thes_ fou_ ar_ al_ day_ o_ risin_ wind_

5-1. In attacking with fire, one should be prepared to meet five possible developments: 6.
5-1. I_ attackin_ wit_ fire_ on_ shoul_ b_ prepare_ t_ mee_ fiv_ possibl_ developments_ 6_

5-2. (1) When fire breaks out inside to enemy's camp, respond at once with an attack from without.
5-2. (1_ Whe_ fir_ break_ ou_ insid_ t_ enemy'_ camp_ respon_ a_ onc_ wit_ a_ attac_ fro_ without_

7. (2) If there is an outbreak of fire, but the enemy's soldiers remain quiet, bide your time and do not attack.
7. (2_ I_ ther_ i_ a_ outbrea_ o_ fire_ bu_ th_ enemy'_ soldier_ remai_ quiet_ bid_ you_ tim_ an_ d_ no_ attack_

8. (3) When the force of the flames has reached its height, follow it up with an attack, if that is practicable; if not, stay where you are.
8. (3_ Whe_ th_ forc_ o_ th_ flame_ ha_ reache_ it_ height_ follo_ i_ u_ wit_ a_ attack_ i_ tha_ i_ practicable_ i_ not_ sta_ wher_ yo_ are_

9. (4) If it is possible to make an assault with fire from without, do not wait for it to break out within, but deliver your attack at a favorable moment.
9. (4_ I_ i_ i_ possibl_ t_ mak_ a_ assaul_ wit_ fir_ fro_ without_ d_ no_ wai_ fo_ i_ t_ brea_ ou_ within_ bu_ delive_ you_ attac_ a_ _ favorabl_ moment_

10-1. (5) When you start a fire, be to windward of it.
10-1. (5_ Whe_ yo_ star_ _ fire_ b_ t_ windwar_ o_ it_

10-2. Do not attack from the leeward.
10-2. D_ no_ attac_ fro_ th_ leeward_

11. A wind that rises in the daytime lasts long, but a night breeze soon falls.
11. _ win_ tha_ rise_ i_ th_ daytim_ last_ long_ bu_ _ nigh_ breez_ soo_ falls_

12. In every army, the five developments connected with fire must be known, the movements of the stars calculated, and a watch kept for the proper days.
12. I_ ever_ army_ th_ fiv_ development_ connecte_ wit_ fir_ mus_ b_ known_ th_ movement_ o_ th_ star_ calculated_ an_ _ watc_ kep_ fo_ th_ prope_ days_

13. Hence those who use fire as an aid to the attack show intelligence; those who use water as an aid to the attack gain an accession of strength.
13. Henc_ thos_ wh_ us_ fir_ a_ a_ ai_ t_ th_ attac_ sho_ intelligence_ thos_ wh_ us_ wate_ a_ a_ ai_ t_ th_ attac_ gai_ a_ accessio_ o_ strength_

14. By means of water, an enemy may be intercepted, but not robbed of all his belongings.
14. B_ mean_ o_ water_ a_ enem_ ma_ b_ intercepted_ bu_ no_ robbe_ o_ al_ hi_ belongings_

15. Unhappy is the fate of one who tries to win his battles and succeed in his attacks without cultivating the spirit of enterprise; for the result is waste of time and general stagnation.
15. Unhapp_ i_ th_ fat_ o_ on_ wh_ trie_ t_ wi_ hi_ battle_ an_ succee_ i_ hi_ attack_ withou_ cultivatin_ th_ spiri_ o_ enterprise_ fo_ th_ resul_ i_ wast_ o_ tim_ an_ genera_ stagnation_

16. Hence the saying: The enlightened ruler lays his plans well ahead; the good general cultivates his resources.
16. Henc_ th_ saying_ Th_ enlightene_ rule_ lay_ hi_ plan_ wel_ ahead_ th_ goo_ genera_ cultivate_ hi_ resources_

17. Move not unless you see an advantage; use not your troops unless there is something to be gained; fight not unless the position is critical.
17. Mov_ no_ unles_ yo_ se_ a_ advantage_ us_ no_ you_ troop_ unles_ ther_ i_ somethin_ t_ b_ gained_ figh_ no_ unles_ th_ positio_ i_ critical_

18. No ruler should put troops into the field merely to gratify his own spleen; no general should fight a battle simply out of pique.
18. N_ rule_ shoul_ pu_ troop_ int_ th_ fiel_ merel_ t_ gratif_ hi_ ow_ spleen_ n_ genera_ shoul_ figh_ _ battl_ simpl_ ou_ o_ pique_

19. If it is to your advantage, make a forward move; if not, stay where you are.
19. I_ i_ i_ t_ you_ advantage_ mak_ _ forwar_ move_ i_ not_ sta_ wher_ yo_ are_

20. Anger may in time change to gladness; vexation may be succeeded by content.
20. Ange_ ma_ i_ tim_ chang_ t_ gladness_ vexatio_ ma_ b_ succeede_ b_ content_

21. But a kingdom that has once been destroyed can never come again into being; nor can the dead ever be brought back to life.
21. Bu_ _ kingdo_ tha_ ha_ onc_ bee_ destroye_ ca_ neve_ com_ agai_ int_ being_ no_ ca_ th_ dea_ eve_ b_ brough_ bac_ t_ life_

22-1. Hence the enlightened ruler is heedful, and the good general full of caution.
22-1. Henc_ th_ enlightene_ rule_ i_ heedful_ an_ th_ goo_ genera_ ful_ o_ caution_

22-2. This is the way to keep a country at peace and an army intact.
22-2. Thi_ i_ th_ wa_ t_ kee_ _ countr_ a_ peac_ an_ a_ arm_ intact_
